About Us

Mission

  • - To revitalize American cities and communities through entrepreneurship.
  • - To enable our best and brightest to create new opportunities for themselves and others.
  • - To restore the culture of achievement to include value-creation, risk and reward, and the common good.

Credo

1. My career is a choice that indicates my values.

"I believe there’s a basic solution to our country’s economic and social problems. We need to get our smart people building things (again)." -Andrew Yang, Founder of Venture for America

2. There is no courage without risk.

"In every career and personal decision that I make, I strive to live by this Frederick Buechner quote: “Vocation is where our greatest passion meets the world's greatest need.” I want to be someone who builds things, helps to create jobs in the community and embraces intelligent risk." -Alexa Wilcox, 2014 Fellow

3. Value creation is how I measure achievement.

"While many others measure achievement through the traditional metrics of money and status, I seek to add as much value as possible to the people and the communities around me because that will bring me the most happiness." -Mitch Rubin, 2014 Fellow

4. I will create opportunities for myself and others.

"My vision is that I will be able to use my skills and successes to enrich the lives of others, helping people create and take advantage of opportunities for themselves." -Katherine Robinson, 2014 Fellow

5. I will act with integrity in all things.

"The entrepreneurial spirit may be broadly defined – some see it as starting a business, others as leading new initiatives, still more as approaching problems in non-traditional ways. I personally see entrepreneurship as one’s passion for and action towards effecting positive lasting change in the world. Whether this change is in an organization, the business world, or anywhere else, in order to accomplish this goal, entrepreneurs must act with integrity everywhere we go else the changes we make will be damage the world or be too fleeting to be valuable." -Keith Porter, 2014 Fellow

Goal

The goal is that a substantial proportion of VFA Fellows will become successful entrepreneurs, preferably rooted in the communities to which they are assigned. Venture for America’s purpose is job generation – our immediate goal is to generate 100,000 new U.S. jobs by 2025 by helping growth companies expand and training a critical mass of our top graduates to themselves become business builders and job creators.
